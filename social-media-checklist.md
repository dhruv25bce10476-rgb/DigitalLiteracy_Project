# Social Media Do's and Don'ts — A College Student's Guide

> This is a checklist for responsible social media use as a college student.

---

## Do's

| # | Do | Why It Matters |
|---|-----|----------------|
| 1 | **Think before you post** — ask yourself: "Would I be comfortable if my professor or a future employer saw this?" | Your digital footprint is permanent. Posts can resurface years later during background checks. |
| 2 | **Keep your profiles professional** — Always use a clear professional photo, accurate bio, and avoid offensive usernames. | First impressions online start with your profile. Recruiters always check social media before recruiting. |
| 3 | **Verify information before sharing** — check the source and date of any news or claim before reposting. | Sharing misinformation, even unintentionally, damages your credibility and spreads harm. |
| 4 | **Use strong privacy settings** — review who can see your posts, friend list, and personal information. |As it protects you from stalking, identity theft, and unwanted contact. |
| 5 | **Be respectful in discussions** — Only disagree with ideas, not people. Avoid personal attacks and aggressive language. | Online arguments escalate quickly. It is good to maintain dignity as it reflects maturity. |

---

## Don'ts

| # | Don't | Why It's Harmful |
|---|-------|------------------|
| 1 | **Don't share personal details publicly** — avoid posting your phone number, home address, class schedule, or hostel room number. | These acts could expose you to stalking, phishing, and real-world safety risks. |
| 2 | **Don't engage in cyberbullying or trolling** — never mock, harass, or target anyone online, even as a "joke." | Cyberbullying is a punishable offence under the IT Act in India and causes serious mental harm. |
| 3 | **Don't accept friend requests from strangers** — especially accounts with no mutual connections or suspicious profiles. | Fake accounts are often used for catfishing, scamming, or data harvesting. |
| 4 | **Don't post in anger or under emotional distress** — step away from the screen before responding to provocative content. | Impulsive posts can damage relationships, reputation, and even lead to legal consequences. |
| 5 | **Don't share exam answers, internal documents, or copyrighted material** — This also includes leaked question papers and pirated content. | These acts violates academic integrity policies and copyright law. Can result in disciplinary action which can affect your career. |
